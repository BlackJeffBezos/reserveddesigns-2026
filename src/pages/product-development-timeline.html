Product Development Process – 8 Complete Stages

Stage 8Ms Framework Application Design Considerations
(Environment / People / Pets / Weather / Physics / Chemistry / Others)		 Business/Product Analysis Techniques (Infused/Applied) Potential Business/Product Failures
1. Idea Generation
Customer feedback, market research, competition analysis, creative sessions
  • Man: Cross-functional teams, diverse experts
  • Machine: Ideation tools, AI assistants, whiteboards
  • Method: Brainstorming, SCAMPER, mind mapping
  • Material: Market reports, survey data, trend docs
  • Measurement: Idea count, novelty ratings
  • Mother Nature: Eco-conscious idea origins
  • Money: Budget for workshops & research
  • Maintenance: Ongoing creativity skill training
  • Environment: Sustainable concepts prioritized
  • People: User needs, ergonomics, inclusivity
  • Pets: Pet safety/behavior (if relevant)
  • Weather: Climate-resilient product ideas
  • Physics: Basic force/durability principles
  • Chemistry: Non-toxic, stable materials
  • Others: Cultural fit, ethics, accessibility
  • SWOT Analysis (early high-level)
  • PESTLE Analysis
  • Porter's Five Forces
  • Market Gap / Trend Analysis
  • Customer Journey Mapping
  • Brainstorming & Affinity Diagrams
  • Ignoring diverse feedback → narrow/biased ideas
  • Weak competition analysis → copycat product
  • Poor market research → trend mismatch
  • Unstructured sessions → low-quality concepts
  • Early ethical or environmental blindness
2. Idea Screening
Assess viability, market potential, technical feasibility, alignment with customer objectives, choose promising solutions
  • Man: Subject-matter experts, gatekeepers
  • Machine: Scoring software, decision matrices
  • Method: Weighted scoring, multi-criteria decision
  • Material: Compiled idea dossiers
  • Measurement: Feasibility & attractiveness scores
  • Mother Nature: Environmental impact screening
  • Money: Rough cost/benefit estimates
  • Maintenance: Refine screening criteria over time
  • Environment: High ecological risk ideas eliminated
  • People: Safety & usability for target users
  • Pets: Animal welfare compliance
  • Weather: Performance in extreme conditions
  • Physics: Preliminary engineering viability
  • Chemistry: Material compatibility/allergens
  • Others: Regulatory, scalability, brand fit
  • SWOT Analysis (idea-specific)
  • PESTLE Analysis (filtering)
  • Risk Assessment Matrix
  • Kano Model (must-be vs. delighters)
  • Feasibility Matrix / Scorecard
  • Competitive Benchmarking
  • Weak screening → pursue unviable ideas
  • Bias toward favorites → ignore data
  • Technical feasibility overlooked
  • Misalignment with strategy/brand
  • Cultural or global market blind spots
3. Concept Development
Test with potential customers/target market to ensure they resonate
  • Man: Designers, marketers, researchers
  • Machine: CAD, Figma, 3D rendering tools
  • Method: Concept iteration, feedback loops
  • Material: Storyboards, mockups, digital prototypes
  • Measurement: Customer preference / NPS scores
  • Mother Nature: Green design integration
  • Money: Concept testing budget
  • Maintenance: Rapid refinement cycles
  • Environment: Sustainable & circular design
  • People: Intuitive use, emotional appeal
  • Pets: Safe & engaging for animals
  • Weather: Exposure resistance concepts
  • Physics: Balance, mechanics modeling
  • Chemistry: Surface/material feel
  • Others: Aesthetics, delight factors
  • SWOT (concept level)
  • PESTLE (trend alignment)
  • Concept Testing Surveys
  • Focus Groups & Interviews
  • Perceptual Mapping
  • Value Proposition Canvas
  • Low resonance → market rejection
  • Insufficient customer input
  • No iteration → stale concepts
  • Missing emotional/niche appeal
  • Privacy or ethical concerns ignored
4. Business Analysis
Production cost, pricing strategy, projected sales volume, competitive landscape
  • Man: Finance, marketing, supply chain
  • Machine: Excel, financial modeling tools
  • Method: Forecasting, sensitivity analysis
  • Material: Supplier quotes, benchmark data
  • Measurement: ROI, NPV, break-even
  • Mother Nature: Sustainability cost factors
  • Money: Full financial planning
  • Maintenance: Scenario & risk updates
  • Environment: Lifecycle & disposal costs
  • People: Price elasticity by segment
  • Weather: Supply chain climate risks
  • Physics/Chemistry: Durability impact on COGS
  • Others: Tariffs, currency, inflation
  • SWOT (full business view)
  • PESTLE (economic forecast)
  • Break-Even & Margin Analysis
  • Porter's Five Forces (deep dive)
  • BCG / Ansoff Matrix
  • Financial Projections (NPV/IRR)
  • Cost underestimation → margin erosion
  • Over-optimistic sales forecast
  • Poor competitive positioning
  • Pricing too high/low → lost sales
  • Economic downturns ignored
5. Product Development
Actual product design, prototyping, rapid testing and refinement, to ensure it meet required specifications and quality
  • Man: Engineers, industrial designers
  • Machine: 3D printers, CNC, CAD/CAM
  • Method: Agile sprints, design reviews
  • Material: Prototyping & final-grade materials
  • Measurement: Tolerance, spec compliance
  • Mother Nature: Low-waste prototyping
  • Money: R&D & prototype budget
  • Maintenance: Design iteration tracking
  • Environment: Eco-materials, minimal waste
  • People: Ergonomics, user safety
  • Pets: Non-toxic, chew-resistant (if applicable)
  • Weather: Temperature/humidity resistance
  • Physics: Stress, fatigue, drop simulation
  • Chemistry: Adhesion, corrosion resistance
  • Others: Modularity, repairability
  • SWOT (development focus)
  • PESTLE (tech/regulatory compliance)
  • DFMA (Design for Manufacture/Assembly)
  • FMEA (Failure Modes & Effects)
  • Rapid Prototyping Feedback
  • Technical Design Reviews
  • Spec drift or unmet requirements
  • Material/supply issues
  • Over-engineering → cost explosion
  • Inadequate refinement → quality gaps
  • Physics/chemistry failures in prototypes
6. Testing and Validation
Implement testing tools/methods and design considerations to ensure safety regulations, quality standards and performance as per region/country of target market. Includes alpha and beta testing
  • Man: QA engineers, compliance specialists
  • Machine: Test chambers, drop towers, software
  • Method: ISO/IEC standards, accelerated life testing
  • Material: Production-intent samples
  • Measurement: Pass/fail rates, MTBF
  • Mother Nature: Environmental & lifecycle testing
  • Money: Certification & lab fees
  • Maintenance: Calibration & protocol updates
  • Environment: RoHS, REACH, carbon footprint
  • People: Human factors, accessibility testing
  • Pets: Safety & non-toxicity validation
  • Weather: IP rating, thermal cycling
  • Physics: Vibration, shock, load tests
  • Chemistry: Leachables, flammability
  • Others: Regional regs (FDA, CE, UL)
  • SWOT (testing strengths/weaknesses)
  • PESTLE (legal & environmental standards)
  • Alpha / Beta Testing Programs
  • QFD (Quality Function Deployment)
  • Regulatory Compliance Audits
  • User Acceptance Testing (UAT)
  • Missed critical defects → recalls
  • Regional regulation non-compliance
  • Insufficient beta feedback
  • Environmental/chemical hazards overlooked
  • Testing budget/time cuts
7. Commercialisation
Marketing, sales, distribution to introduce product/business to target audience effectively
  • Man: Marketing, sales, logistics teams
  • Machine: CRM, e-commerce platforms
  • Method: Go-to-market plan, launch campaign
  • Material: Packaging, POS materials
  • Measurement: Launch KPIs (reach, sell-through)
  • Mother Nature: Sustainable packaging
  • Money: Launch & promo budget
  • Maintenance: Supply chain monitoring
  • Environment: Green claims & packaging
  • People: Targeted messaging by segment
  • Pets: Specialized pet channels
  • Weather: Seasonal timing
  • Physics: Safe transport & handling
  • Chemistry: Shelf-life stability
  • Others: Localization, digital reach
  • SWOT (launch strategy)
  • PESTLE (market timing)
  • 4Ps / 7Ps Marketing Mix
  • STP (Segmentation, Targeting, Positioning)
  • Competitor Response Planning
  • Launch Risk Assessment
  • Weak marketing → low awareness
  • Distribution bottlenecks/stockouts
  • Pricing mismatch with value
  • Competitor counter-moves
  • Poor launch timing (seasonal/off-trend)
8. Post-Launch Monitoring and Improvement
Following the product launch and trends. Collect customer feedback, identify issues, areas of improvement to guide products/businesses updates and enhancements. Continuing improvement
  • Man: Customer support, data analysts
  • Machine: Analytics dashboards, CRM
  • Method: Kaizen, Lean continuous improvement
  • Material: Feedback data, returns analysis
  • Measurement: NPS, CSAT, churn rate
  • Mother Nature: Real-world eco-performance
  • Money: Update & iteration budget
  • Maintenance: Lifecycle management system
  • Environment: Actual sustainability tracking
  • People: Ongoing usability feedback
  • Pets: Long-term safety monitoring
  • Weather: Field performance in real conditions
  • Physics: Wear, fatigue analysis
  • Chemistry: Degradation over time
  • Others: Trend adaptation, firmware updates
  • SWOT (periodic refresh)
  • PESTLE (ongoing monitoring)
  • NPS & Voice of Customer
  • Root Cause Analysis (Fishbone, 5 Whys)
  • Product Lifecycle Management (PLM)
  • Agile Retrospectives & A/B Testing
  • Ignoring feedback → unresolved pain points
  • No adaptation → product obsolescence
  • Quality decay without fixes
  • Missed recall signals
  • Underfunding of updates
