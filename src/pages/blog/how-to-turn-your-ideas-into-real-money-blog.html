The Icons Who Paved the Way

Our nation's history is filled with inventors who saw a problem and refused to look away, often starting with nothing more than a prototype or a sketch.

  • Ferdinand Chauvier (1974): Invented the Kreepy Krauly in Springs — the world’s first automatic pool cleaner, which became a global export success.
  • George Pratley & Frank Robinson (1948): Created Pratley’s Putty, an epoxy so strong it was used by Apollo 11 astronauts to fix a drill bit on the Moon.
  • Mashudu Tshifularo (2019): Performed the world’s first 3D-printed middle ear bone transplant at University of Pretoria.
  • Neo Hutiri (2015): Invented the Pelebox, a smart locker system that lets patients collect chronic medication in minutes instead of hours.
  • Percy Tucker (1971): Created Computicket, the world's first computerized ticketing system.
  • Vivian Alberts (2000s): Developed economical thin-film solar technology that lowered global production costs.
  • Gabriella Mogale (2018): As a student, invented fire-proof shack insulation to address deadly informal settlement fires.
  • Henri Johnson (1992): Invented the first handheld speed gun used in global sports like cricket and tennis.

Step 1: Protect It (But Not How You Think)

Forget the myth that you need a R150,000+ patent before talking to anyone. Smart South African inventors use a R590 Provisional Patent Application (PPA) through CIPC — that gives you “patent pending” status for 12 months. That’s enough protection to start shopping the idea safely.
